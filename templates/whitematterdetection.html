White Matter Change Detection

  • Category: Computer Vision
  • Client: Alkahest, Inc
  • Project date: 12 January, 2023

Introduction

The problem that I see while working in neurological research is this state of constant bias, whether it's selection bias or sampling bias. Due to the complexities and near undetectable, but significant, changes in central nervous system processes, it is very necessary to automate the region selection. This tool is just one of the possible ways to minimize human-data interaction by having a system choose the parts of the brain that is "interesting".
The visual differences in white matter histological staining (in other words, images under the microscope) is not strong enough to be detected by human eye. By adding in a non-biased computer vision process we can automate the ROIs that may be important to look during certain neurodegenerative states.
I am currently investigating the use of a multi-class classification algorithm using TensorFlow/Keras by Transfer Learning from Tensorhub's Mobilenet_v2. Hopefully with this mult-class labeler, I can identify brains with similar structure to a model with a certain indication of interest. The model optimization process is currently undergoing as data is still being collected and labeled.
