Who Am I

  • just a bit more about my experiences

Greetings! My name is Johnny Sin, I was born in South Korea then spent my childhood in LA. Throughout my high school I was part of the Palos Verdes Institute of Technology, which sparked my passion for engineering. Later, I joined a Cell Signaling Proteomics group at UCLA which made me want to learn more about the human body and how I can improve lives with the technologies that I want to build. So, I graduated UC Berkeley with a degree in Molecular and Cell Biology, and during my time in college, I tried to create a start-up with my friends. As the product engineer, my job was to build the architecture for the device that would ultimately harvest stem cells non-invasively using Autodesk Maya.

Immediately after, I worked in a neuroscience lab studying multiple sclerosis in patients for four years then moved to industry. Working with patients through visual examinations and tests, I saw how important the accuracy in data analysis needs to be. Currently I am working at Alkahest to study age-related neurological diseases, essentially searching for the “fountain of youth”. Throughout my career, I trained as a researcher with more than five years of experience analyzing large clinical and experimental data. The most fun I had during my work was discovering an unexpected story hidden in the mountains of biological data. Creatively executing statistical tests to model neurological disease was the part of my job that I truly enjoyed. Although working full-time, I enrolled in classes to further hone my programming skills, and currently, machine learning occupy the majority of my free time. Juggling between work and classes, I love creating digital animations to provide my students, patients, and fellow colleagues a more immersive learning experience and using graphic design to create T-shirts and logos!
