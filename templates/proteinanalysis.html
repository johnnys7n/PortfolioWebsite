Machine Learning for Characterizing Aging with Plasma Proteins

  • Category: Predictive Protein Analysis
  • Client: Current Company/Personal
  • Project date: 15 July, 2023
  • Project URL: Click Here For Github Link

Introduction

Currently at my company, we specialize in looking at the plasma proteins of various neurodegenerative models and we use this plasma science to determine potential therapeutics. As a bit of background for the non-biology audience, plasma is part of the blood and there are enormous variability in protein composition, as is the case in other tissues such as the brain, kidney, and even the skin. Adding to this variability, age and disease, along with other demographic and perhaps regional factors, are influential in the composition of the plasma proteome.
What I am trying to accomplish with this is to identify potentially interesting proteins amongst the thousands of proteins to better identify drug target candidates especially in age-related diseases. Perhaps, with the goal of predicting age using the various composition of these proteins (but this is currently under research!). I am very passionate about creating new possible data investigation methodologies to common problems in life sciences research, and I am hoping this confirmation analysis using unsupervised clustering combined with predictive modeling will help elucidate our target focus.
I have also created a application using the HuggingFace Gradio framework to deploy an early-stage machine learning model to predict age through plasma protein composition. The values input are in pg/ml concentration derived from fluorescence intensity.
